CHANDRAYAAN-3

Chandrayaan-3 is India's third lunar exploration mission, developed by ISRO. It achieved a historic milestone by successfully landing near the Moon's south pole, a region unexplored by previous missions.

Historic Landing

Chandrayaan-3 marked India's successful soft landing near the Moon's south pole, making it the first mission to achieve this feat.

Key Specifications

- Mission Type: Lunar Exploration
- Weight: 1,752 kg
- Objective: Soft landing and rover exploration

Scientific Achievements

Equipped with advanced scientific instruments, Chandrayaan-3 studied lunar soil composition, thermal properties, and seismic activity.

Importance for Space Exploration

Chandrayaan-3 has propelled India into the elite club of nations mastering lunar landings. It underscores the potential of cost-effective space missions and provides valuable insights into the Moon's south pole, paving the way for future manned missions and lunar resource utilization.
