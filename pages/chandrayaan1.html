CHANDRAYAAN-1

Chandrayaan-1 was Indiaâ€™s first lunar exploration mission, marking ISRO's entry into interplanetary exploration. Launched in 2008, the mission achieved a major milestone by discovering water molecules on the Moon's surface, revolutionizing our understanding of lunar science.

Key Objectives

- Study lunar surface composition.
- Map the Moon in 3D using high-resolution imagery.
- Search for water ice and minerals on the lunar poles.

Major Achievements

Chandrayaan-1 detected water molecules on the Moon, a groundbreaking discovery that has inspired lunar exploration programs worldwide.

Mission Facts

- Launched: October 22, 2008.
- Weight: 1,380 kg.
- Duration: Originally planned for 2 years but completed 312 days.
- Instruments: 11 payloads from ISRO and international space agencies.

Legacy

Chandrayaan-1 set the stage for India's subsequent lunar missions and firmly established ISRO as a global player in space exploration. Its discovery of water on the Moon redefined scientific goals for lunar exploration.
