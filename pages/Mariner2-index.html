Mariner 2:First to Venus

What was Mariner 2?

Mariner 2 was the first spacecraft to successfully perform a flyby of another planet, achieving this historic milestone with its encounter with Venus on December 14, 1962. Part of NASA’s Mariner program, it provided groundbreaking data about Venus and interplanetary space, paving the way for future planetary exploration.

Mission: Mariner 2

Launch Date: August 27, 1962

Objective: Study Venus’s atmosphere, temperature, and surface conditions and Investigate interplanetary space, including solar wind, cosmic rays, and magnetic fields.

Orbit

On December 14, 1962, Mariner 2 flew within 34,773 km (21,607 miles) of Venus, becoming the first spacecraft to send back data from another planet.

Outcome

Mariner 2 confirmed that Venus has an extremely hot surface temperature of about 425°C (800°F), making it inhospitable for life as we know it, and found that its atmosphere is primarily composed of carbon dioxide with high surface pressures. It made the first successful measurements of the solar wind, proving its continuous existence, and detected no significant magnetic field around Venus.

Legacy

It provided critical insights into Venus and interplanetary space, laying the foundation for future missions to study Venus and other planets.
