Yuri:First Human in space

Who wwas Yuri Gagarin

The first human in space was Yuri Gagarin, a Soviet cosmonaut. He achieved this historic milestone on April 12, 1961, aboard the spacecraft Vostok 1. This mission made him the first person to orbit Earth and marked a pivotal moment in the Space Race.

Mission: Vostok 1

Launch Date:April 12, 1961

Objective: Its primary mission was to demonstrate the feasibility of human spaceflight by safely sending a human into orbit and returning them to Earth. The mission aimed to test the human body's ability to survive in space, validate the functionality of the automated Vostok spacecraft

Orbit

Gagarin completed one orbit around Earth, reaching a maximum altitude of 327 kilometers (203 miles).

The Journey

The spacecraft traveled at a speed of 27,400 km/h (17,000 mph), completing the orbit in 108 minutes.

Outcome

This mission demonstrated the feasibility of human spaceflight and was a major victory for the Soviet Union in the Space Race.

Legacy

Yuri Gagarin's flight aboard Vostok 1 had a profound impact, marking a turning point in human history by proving that space travel was possible for humans.
