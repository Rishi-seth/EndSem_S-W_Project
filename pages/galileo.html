Galileo Mission

The Galileo mission revolutionized our understanding of Jupiter and its moons. Launched in 1989, Galileo provided detailed data on the gas giant, unveiling its intricate magnetosphere and discovering evidence of subsurface oceans on Europa.

Mission Highlights

- First spacecraft to orbit Jupiter.
- Discovered volcanic activity on Io.
- Gathered evidence of liquid water oceans beneath Europa's icy crust.

Scientific Impact

The mission significantly advanced planetary science, influencing future missions to outer planets.

Legacy

Galileo's discoveries inspire ongoing exploration of Jupiter and its moons, with missions like Juno and the upcoming Europa Clipper.
