STS-107 Mission

STS-107 was the final mission of Space Shuttle Columbia, dedicated to scientific research in space. Tragically, it ended in disaster upon re-entry on February 1, 2003, marking a turning point for NASA’s safety protocols.

Mission Objectives

- Conduct over 80 experiments in microgravity.
- Research combustion, fluid dynamics, and material properties.
- Study human physiology in space environments.

Legacy of STS-107

The mission’s tragic loss led to significant changes in spacecraft safety and operational procedures. It remains a vital lesson in human spaceflight history.

Legacy

The STS-107 mission serves as a reminder of the risks of space exploration, inspiring innovation and caution in future endeavors.
