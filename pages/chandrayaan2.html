CHANDRAYAAN-2

Chandrayaan-2 was India’s ambitious lunar exploration mission, designed to explore the Moon’s surface and study its composition. Though its lander did not achieve a soft landing, its orbiter continues to provide valuable data about the Moon.

Orbiter Success

The Chandrayaan-2 orbiter remains operational and provides detailed imagery and scientific data of the Moon’s surface.

Key Specifications

- Mission Type: Orbiter + Lander + Rover
- Weight: 3,850 kg
- Objective: Explore lunar topography and water ice deposits

Lessons Learned

Though the Vikram lander did not achieve a soft landing, the mission provided valuable engineering insights, aiding Chandrayaan-3.

Legacy

Chandrayaan-2 represents a milestone in India's space exploration journey, showcasing ISRO's capabilities in interplanetary missions. Despite setbacks, it continues to inspire advancements in lunar exploration and builds a foundation for future missions like Chandrayaan-3.
