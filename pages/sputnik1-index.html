Sputnik 1:First in space legacy

What was Sputnik 1?

Sputnik 1 was the first artificial satellite to orbit Earth, launched by the Soviet Union. Its launch marked the beginning of the space age and was a major milestone in human exploration of space.

Mission: Sputnik 1

Launch Date: October 4, 1957

Objective: Its primary mission was to test the feasibility of launching an artificial satellite into orbit and to gather basic data about the Earth's upper atmosphere.

Orbit

Sputnik 1 orbited Earth at an altitude ranging from 215 to 939 kilometers (134 to 583 miles) and completed one orbit every 96 minutes.

The Journey

It operated for 21 days, broadcasting radio signals until its batteries died. Sputnik 1 reentered Earth's atmosphere and burned up on January 4, 1958.

Outcome

Sputnik 1's successful launch demonstrated the Soviet Union's technological capabilities and advanced understanding of rocketry.

Legacy

The achievement inspired scientists and engineers worldwide, eventually leading to human spaceflights and lunar landings.
