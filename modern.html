FALCON 9

FALCON 9 is the world's first orbital-class reusable rocket, developed by SpaceX. Its revolutionary design enables the reusability of boosters, reducing the cost of space access.

Reusability

Falcon 9's ability to land and reuse its first stage is a game-changer in space exploration.

Specifications

- Height: 70 meters
- Diameter: 3.7 meters
- Payload to Low Earth Orbit (LEO): 22,800 kg

Historical Launch

On December 21, 2015, Falcon 9 successfully landed its first-stage booster back on Earth, marking a pivotal moment in reusable rocket technology.

Legacy

Falcon 9 revolutionized space travel, paving the way for ambitious projects like Starship and Starlink. Its reusability has drastically reduced costs, bringing humanity closer to interplanetary exploration.
